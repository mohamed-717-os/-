Chicken Udon Noodle Soup

History Of Udon Noodles

The exact origin of udon is unknown and although there are several theories,
the oldest one claims that udon came from China in the 700s.
It became widely popular to the public during the Edo era in the 1600s. Much like udon,
soba is also said to come from China in the 700s but did not gain popularity until the 1700s.

How To Make Udon Noodles?

Ingredients amount
chicken stock 1 ½ (32 fluid ounce) containers
minced fresh ginger root 1 tablespoon
chili powder 1 teaspoon
garlic, minced 1 clove
large cooked skinless,
boneless chicken breast,
chopped		 1
bok choy, chopped 1 head
dried shiitake mushrooms ¼ cup
dried udon noodles 2 packages (7 ounce)
mung bean sprouts ½ cup
green onion, sliced diagonally 1
dried minced onion 2 tablespoons
chopped fresh cilantro 1 tablespoon

Directions

  1. Bring chicken stock, ginger, chili powder,
    and garlic to a boil in a pot over medium-high heat.
    Add chicken, bok choy, and mushrooms;
    reduce heat and simmer lightly for 3 minutes.
    Add noodles and cook for 4 minutes.
  2. Ladle soup mixture into bowls. Garnish with bean sprouts,
    green onion, dried onion, and cilantro.
